Welcome to the 2015 Smartest Loser Competition!

Welcome, and congratulations on taking the necessary steps to achieve better health! We’re excited you’ve decided to make a commitment to embrace a more active lifestyle.

The Smartest Loser is a 10-week wellness program designed to help you become more physically fit and focus on proper eating habits. Each activity allows you to earn points each week. Your ultimate goal is to accrue 1870 points at the end of the 10 weeks.

By participating in The Smartest Loser you are making a commitment to improving your health. Actively taking charge of your personal health can have a dramatic impact on your daily life and potentially lead to an improved mood, increased energy and decreased stress...you may even strengthen your heart too.

New this year

  • Team Steps competition: In the competitive spirit of eMoney we’re including a step challenge for teams. The average steps per week between teams over the 10 weeks will result in a healthy catered lunch for the winning team! (Step counts will not be used in the individual score this year.)
  • Access to a nutritionist: We held a kick-off meeting with a nutritionist to help you start Smartest Loser right. You can now arrange for weekly visits with our in-house nutritionist. (Also, 6 nutritionist visits are included in our health plan.)
  • Body fat percentage is also being tracked this year! If you don't want to step on a scale or don't have pouds to lose, here's your chance to jump into the competition! Focus on toning up and still have a chance to win.
    The Wellness team will take your greatest loss of body weight percentage lost AND body fat percentage percentage lost, and will add that to your weekly score.

Food Tracking

Need a way to make sure you're getting the right intake for your day? Track your food or check your caloric intake with these suggested apps:

  • MyFitnessPal
  • Fitbit
  • LoseIt

Use FreeDieting.com to discover what your ideal daily calorie range is for fat loss (but don’t use the ‘extreme’ number). Then, keep your daily intake in that range. Remember—too few calories can actually slow your metabolism and prevent weight loss. And don’t forget that what you eat is just as important as how much you eat. Quality of calories matter!
